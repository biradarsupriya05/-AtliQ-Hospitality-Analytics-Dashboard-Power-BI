# 🏨 AtliQ-Hospitality-Analytics-Dashboard-Power-BI

### 📌 Problem Statement

AtliQ Grands, a leading chain of luxury hotels in India, has been losing market share and revenue due to rising competition and poor strategic decisions. 
To improve business performance, the management aims to leverage Business Intelligence and data-driven insights, despite not having an in-house analytics team.

### ⭐ Key Hospitality Metrics

| **Metric** | **Description** |
|------------|-----------------|
| **ADR (Average Daily Rate)** | Revenue earned per occupied room. |
| **RevPAR (Revenue per Available Room)** | Revenue generated per available room. |
| **Occupancy %** | Percentage of available rooms that were occupied. |
| **Realisation %** | Percentage of bookings successfully converted into stays. |
| **DBRN (Daily Booked Room Nights)** | Average number of room nights booked per day. |
| **DSRN (Daily Sold Room Nights)** | Average number of room nights sold per day. |
| **DURN (Daily Utilized Room Nights)** | Average number of room nights actually utilized per day. |
| **Cancellation %** | Percentage of bookings that were cancelled. |

### 📊 Overall Business Insights for Stakeholders

-	Generated ₹1.71B in total revenue with an overall RevPAR of ₹7,347, ADR of ₹12,696, and Occupancy Rate of 57.87%, indicating healthy business performance. 

-	The Luxury category contributed 61.61% of total revenue, making it the company's primary revenue driver compared to the Business category. 

-	Weekend performance outperformed weekdays in terms of Occupancy, ADR, RevPAR, and Realisation, highlighting stronger customer demand during weekends.
  
-	AtliQ Palace, AtliQ Exotica, and AtliQ City recorded the highest booking volumes, while AtliQ Seasons consistently underperformed with the lowest bookings and customer ratings.
  
-	AtliQ Blu achieved the highest customer rating (4.0), reflecting better guest satisfaction and service quality.
  
-	The company recorded 135K total bookings, of which 94K were successful, while 33K bookings were cancelled, resulting in a 24.83% cancellation rate that impacts realized revenue.

-	Mumbai recorded the highest number of cancelled bookings, making it a priority market for improving booking retention and reducing cancellations.
   
-	Third-party booking platforms generated the majority of bookings, while Direct Online and Direct Offline channels contributed comparatively fewer bookings, indicating an opportunity to strengthen direct sales. 

-	Elite room class accounted for the highest share of cancelled bookings, suggesting the need to review pricing, booking policies, or customer expectations for premium rooms.
  
-	Weekly KPI trends show relatively stable RevPAR with fluctuations in ADR and Occupancy, providing valuable insights for pricing and demand management.
  
-	Strategic Focus: Reduce booking cancellations, improve underperforming properties, increase direct bookings, and leverage high-performing luxury hotels to maximize revenue and profitability.
